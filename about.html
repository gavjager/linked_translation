What is this Thing Anyways?

It is first and foremost a translation website. It is currently under construction as a final project for my German 472 class at the University of Michigan. If I like how it turns out, I might continue to develop it!

I'm choosing to call it a "linked-dynamic" translation, because it uses HTML and Javascript to link poetry and commentary and make the actual text of the poems dynamic. (There's some CSS to make it pretty, too, or at least marginally better than plaintext).

What's so Interesting About It?

The goal is to explore a new way of translating. Good translation should stand on its own as a work of art. It should awake an echo of the original,1 and function on its own terms in the target language. Translation also cannot escape its role as commentary on the original work and on the process of translation itself. Performing this task artfully relies on an economy of language and information: too little, and the work becomes either cryptic – an exercise in "translation-ese" – or disconnected from the original; too much, and the translation is bogged down, broken up, and becomes merely commentary and disassembly of the original.

With the internet, we have a new medium, and new rules. Information is more accesible, and can be presented in new ways. Language can be used in ways that were not previously possible. We have, in effect, a new economy of language and information, one that lends itself well to the process and product of translation.
